# Philosophy

These principles are mandatory when producing an FDD.

## Simplicity First

- Favor the simplest design that satisfies the stated requirements.
- Avoid speculative abstractions and future-proofing without a concrete need.
- If a more complex option is selected, explain why the simpler one is insufficient.

## Boundary Discipline

- Keep responsibilities narrow and explicit.
- Prefer clear contracts over implicit coupling.
- Place cross-cutting concerns behind documented interfaces where possible.

## Operational Clarity

- Favor observability and debuggability over cleverness.
- Ensure failures are explicit, scoped, and recoverable.
